Project Overview

The project aimed to strengthen technical capacity across Bhutan's DrukREN member institutions. With over 200 connected agencies, many lacked the skills and infrastructure to fully utilize DrukREN's high-speed network. Through Train-the-Trainers (TTT) programs and capacity-building workshops, the project focused on CCNA, CCNP, IPv6 deployment, and network automation.

Challenges & Future Directions

Challenges Encountered

Limited Technical Skills

Many new DrukREN member institutions lacked skilled personnel to manage and troubleshoot campus networks effectively.

Lack of Local Training Resources

Absence of structured, in-country technical training made skill development dependent on international programs.

High Dependency on External Trainers

Reliance on overseas trainers increased project costs and made future sustainability challenging.

Budget Constraints

Limited funding restricted the scale of training and number of participants that could be accommodated.

Administrative Delays

Procurement and coordination processes took longer than expected, affecting timely execution of activities.

Infrastructure Gaps

Some institutions lacked necessary hardware and stable connectivity to fully utilize DrukREN services.

Uneven Skill Levels

Varied technical backgrounds made it difficult to deliver uniformly paced training for all attendees.

Future Directions

  • Expand Local Training Programs: Establish regular in-country training sessions to build local expertise and reduce dependence on international trainers
  • Upgrade Campus Infrastructure: Assist institutions in enhancing internal networks for better performance and full DrukREN utilization
  • Encourage Professional Certification: Support staff in achieving certifications like CCNA and CCNP to standardize technical capacity
  • National Training Hub: Create a permanent center to deliver hands-on training and serve as a resource for DrukREN members
  • Peer Learning Networks: Encourage collaboration and knowledge-sharing among institutions to solve common challenges
  • Increase Female Participation: Design inclusive programs to attract more women to networking and technical training opportunities
  • Improve Support Systems: Implement centralized tools and remote support teams for troubleshooting and optimization
